30-Day Advanced Grammar + 3 Phrasal Verbs Speaking Plan

Full month plan with grammar focus, 3 phrasal verbs, a practice prompt, and some suggested questions to practice with for each day.

Week 1: Conditionals & Hypotheticals

Day 1 – Mixed Conditionals (past → present)

Phrasal verbs: give up, figure out, take up
"First, explain mixed conditionals where a past action affects the present. Then, teach me the phrasal verbs **give up**, **figure out**, and **take up**. After that, ask me to create 3 examples from my life using mixed conditionals and include these phrasal verbs. Some suggested questions to practice with: - If you hadn’t given up on something in the past, how would life be different now? - What’s something you wish you had figured out earlier? - If you had taken up a skill earlier, how would it affect your present?"

Day 2 – Mixed Conditionals (present → past)

Phrasal verbs: hold back, carry out, break into
"First, explain mixed conditionals where a present situation affects the past. Then, teach me **hold back**, **carry out**, and **break into**. After that, ask me to create examples. Some suggested questions to practice with: - If you weren’t holding back now, what would you have carried out earlier? - If you were braver, what would you have broken into (a field, a skill) in the past? - How would your life look today if you didn’t hold back so much?"

Day 3 – Third Conditional (regrets/speculation)

Phrasal verbs: break down, turn out, fall through
"First, explain the third conditional. Then, teach me **break down**, **turn out**, and **fall through**. After that, ask me to describe regrets using them. Some suggested questions to practice with: - If your plans hadn’t broken down, how would things have turned out? - If an opportunity hadn’t fallen through, where would you be now? - If you had handled things differently, how would it all have turned out?"

Day 4 – Second Conditional (imagination)

Phrasal verbs: set up, run into, take off
"First, explain the second conditional. Then, teach me **set up**, **run into**, and **take off**. After that, ask me to describe my dream lifestyle. Some suggested questions to practice with: - If you could set up any company, what would it be? - If your idea suddenly took off, how would your life change? - If you ran into your favorite celebrity, what would you say?"

Day 5 – Inverted Conditionals (Had I…, Were I…, Should I…)

Phrasal verbs: bring up, put off, call off
"First, explain inverted conditionals. Then, teach me **bring up**, **put off**, and **call off**. After that, ask me to use them in advice sentences. Some suggested questions to practice with: - Were you to bring up a delicate subject, how would you do it? - Had you not put off an important decision, how would things be different? - Should an event be called off, how would you react?"

Day 6 – Wish + Past Simple / Past Perfect

Phrasal verbs: make up, come across, get over
"First, explain wish structures. Then, teach me **make up**, **come across**, and **get over**. After that, ask me to share present wishes and past regrets. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What do you wish you had made up after an argument? - What do you wish you had come across earlier in life? - What do you wish you could get over more easily?"

Day 7 – If only… (strong wishes/regrets)

Phrasal verbs: look after, find out, give in
"First, explain ‘If only…’. Then, teach me **look after**, **find out**, and **give in**. After that, ask me to create strong wishes/regrets. Some suggested questions to practice with: - If only you had found out something earlier, how would life change? - If only people looked after each other more, what would happen? - If only you hadn’t given in once, how would things be different now?"

Week 2: Advanced Verb Forms

Day 8 – Future Perfect

Phrasal verbs: work out, fall apart, put together
"First, explain the future perfect. Then, teach me **work out**, **fall apart**, and **put together**. After that, ask me to predict achievements by 2035. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What will you have worked out in your career in 10 years? - What will have fallen apart in society by 2050? - What will you have put together by the time you’re 40?"

Day 9 – Future Continuous

Phrasal verbs: hang out, check out, chill out
"First, explain the future continuous. Then, teach me **hang out**, **check out**, and **chill out**. After that, ask me to describe what I’ll be doing this weekend. Some suggested questions to practice with: - Who will you be hanging out with next weekend? - What new place will you be checking out soon? - When will you be chilling out and relaxing next?"

Day 10 – Past Perfect Continuous

Phrasal verbs: take over, run out, hold on
"First, explain the past perfect continuous. Then, teach me **take over**, **run out**, and **hold on**. After that, ask me to tell a story about something that happened after I had been doing something for a long time. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What had been taking over your time before an important event? - What had you been doing when you suddenly ran out of energy? - What had you been holding on to for too long?"

Day 11 – Modals of Deduction (past)

Phrasal verbs: carry on, point out, wind up
"First, explain modals of deduction in the past. Then, teach me **carry on**, **point out**, and **wind up**. After that, ask me to describe a mysterious situation and make guesses. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What must have been carried on in secret in history? - What might someone have pointed out to you that changed your perspective? - How could you have wound up in a completely different life?"

Day 12 – Modals in the Future

Phrasal verbs: cut down, pick up, kick off
"First, explain modals in the future. Then, teach me **cut down**, **pick up**, and **kick off**. After that, ask me to predict future challenges. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What habits will people have to cut down on in the future? - What new skills will people need to pick up? - What global changes will kick off in the next decade?"

Day 13 – Cleft Sentences

Phrasal verbs: bring about, get through, pull off
"First, explain cleft sentences. Then, teach me **bring about**, **get through**, and **pull off**. After that, ask me to retell a story emphasizing certain details. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What event brought about the biggest change in your life? - What helped you get through a hard moment? - What amazing thing have you pulled off?"

Day 14 – Passive with Reporting Verbs

Phrasal verbs: go on, look into, build up
"First, explain passive reporting verbs. Then, teach me **go on**, **look into**, and **build up**. After that, ask me to talk about common beliefs. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What is believed to have gone on behind the scenes in politics? - What should be looked into more by scientists? - What good habits should be built up over time?"

Week 3: Advanced Structures for Fluency

Day 15 – Relative Clauses with prepositions

Phrasal verbs: rely on, back up, check in
"First, explain relative clauses with prepositions. Then, teach me **rely on**, **back up**, and **check in**. After that, ask me to describe someone important. Some suggested questions to practice with: - Who do you rely on the most? - What ideas would you always back up? - Who do you check in with regularly?"

Day 16 – Reduced Relative Clauses

Phrasal verbs: go over, come up with, drop out
"First, explain reduced relative clauses. Then, teach me **go over**, **come up with**, and **drop out**. After that, ask me to describe people I know or admire. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What’s the best idea you’ve ever come up with? - What details do you usually go over carefully? - Why do people drop out of projects or school?"

Day 17 – Participle Clauses

Phrasal verbs: end up, hand in, look back on
"First, explain participle clauses. Then, teach me **end up**, **hand in**, and **look back on**. After that, ask me to tell a story connecting actions. Some suggested questions to practice with: - When have you ended up in an unexpected situation? - What’s the most important thing you’ve ever handed in? - What do you look back on with pride?"

Day 18 – Nominalization

Phrasal verbs: cut off, bring in, lay off
"First, explain nominalization. Then, teach me **cut off**, **bring in**, and **lay off**. After that, ask me to talk about technology. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What invention brought in the biggest change in your life? - How would people feel if they were cut off from the internet? - What company has laid off too many workers recently?"

Day 19 – Inversion for Emphasis

Phrasal verbs: turn down, put up with, speak out
"First, explain inversion for emphasis. Then, teach me **turn down**, **put up with**, and **speak out**. After that, ask me to share extreme experiences. Some suggested questions to practice with: - Never have I put up with such a situation… (finish). - Rarely have I turned down such an opportunity… (explain). - When did you have to speak out about something important?"

Day 20 – Ellipsis & Substitution

Phrasal verbs: take up, fill in, leave out
"First, explain ellipsis and substitution. Then, teach me **take up**, **fill in**, and **leave out**. After that, ask me to talk about hobbies. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What new activity have you taken up recently? - When did you last have to fill in something important? - What details do you sometimes leave out when telling stories?"

Week 4: Polished, Natural English

Day 21 – Fronting

Phrasal verbs: set off, catch up, keep up
"First, explain fronting. Then, teach me **set off**, **catch up**, and **keep up**. After that, ask me to talk about priorities. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What I really want is… (finish). - Who do you enjoy catching up with the most? - How do you keep up with trends or news?"

Day 22 – Reported Speech (advanced)

Phrasal verbs: call off, check in, pass on
"First, explain reported speech. Then, teach me **call off**, **check in**, and **pass on**. After that, ask me to retell a conversation. Some suggested questions to practice with: - Have you ever had an important plan called off? - Who did you check in with recently? - What useful advice was passed on to you?"

Day 23 – Reporting Verbs

Phrasal verbs: find out, look forward to, bring up
"First, explain reporting verbs. Then, teach me **find out**, **look forward to**, and **bring up**. After that, ask me to retell a story. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What surprising thing did you find out recently? - What are you looking forward to this year? - What sensitive topics do people usually bring up?"

Day 24 – Concessive Clauses

Phrasal verbs: run into, sort out, brush up on
"First, explain concessive clauses. Then, teach me **run into**, **sort out**, and **brush up on**. After that, ask me to talk about a challenge I overcame. Some suggested questions to practice with: - Have you ever run into an unexpected problem? - What difficult situation have you sorted out? - What skill should you brush up on soon?"

Day 25 – Expressions with ‘as’

Phrasal verbs: bring up, get along, open up
"First, explain advanced ‘as’ expressions. Then, teach me **bring up**, **get along**, and **open up**. After that, ask me to share opinions. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What topic would you never bring up? - Who do you get along with the best? - When do you usually open up to others?"

Day 26 – Double Comparatives

Phrasal verbs: pick out, cut back, show up
"First, explain double comparatives. Then, teach me **pick out**, **cut back**, and **show up**. After that, ask me to talk about habits. Some suggested questions to practice with: - The more you cut back on something, the better life gets… explain. - The more carefully you pick out things, the happier you are… why? - How does showing up on time change relationships?"

Day 27 – Advanced Linking Devices

Phrasal verbs: go on, break into, turn up
"First, explain advanced linking devices. Then, teach me **go on**, **break into**, and **turn up**. After that, ask me to debate pros/cons. Some suggested questions to practice with: - Not only do people go on using social media, but… (continue). - How difficult is it to break into a new industry? - What do you do when opportunities suddenly turn up?"

Day 28 – Emphatic ‘do’

Phrasal verbs: put together, look back on, step up
"First, explain emphatic ‘do’. Then, teach me **put together**, **look back on**, and **step up**. After that, ask me to insist on opinions. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What’s something you proudly put together? - What do you look back on fondly? - When did you have to step up and take responsibility?"

Day 29 – Mixed Structures Practice

Phrasal verbs: go through, bring out, check out
"First, review all advanced structures. Then, teach me **go through**, **bring out**, and **check out**. After that, ask me to tell a personal story mixing grammar points. Some suggested questions to practice with: - What have you gone through that made you stronger? - Who brings out the best in you? - What new thing have you checked out recently?"

Day 30 – Final Reflection

Phrasal verbs: wrap up, move on, take over
"First, explain how to combine advanced structures naturally. Then, teach me **wrap up**, **move on**, and **take over**. After that, ask me to reflect on my English journey. Some suggested questions to practice with: - How would you wrap up this learning month? - What will you move on to next? - How might English take over more areas of your life?"
That's the full 30-day plan — each day includes a grammar focus, 3 phrasal verbs, a practice prompt, and some suggested questions to practice with.
